Route One

Poland Type D Work Visa

The Polish national (Type D) visa allows non-EU nationals to live and work in Poland for up to 12 months. It is issued by the Polish consulate in your country of residence and requires either a work permit issued by a Polish employer, or a Declaration of Entrusting Work. Poland is one of the fastest-growing economies in Central Europe — and one of the most accessible routes for West African professionals.

Key Facts

Visa Type

National (Type D)

Max Duration

12 months

Consulate Fee

~£80

Processing Time

15–45 days

Schengen Access

Yes (90 days)

Renewable

Yes, in-country

Documents Required

Valid passport (minimum 6 months beyond intended stay)
Work permit issued by the Polish labour authority, or Declaration of Entrusting Work
Signed employment contract or confirmed job offer letter
Proof of accommodation in Poland
Travel health insurance (minimum €30,000 Schengen cover)
Proof of sufficient funds (bank statements, 3 months)
Completed visa application form + biometric photos

Application Timeline

1

Profile Assessment

We review your documents, employment situation, and travel history. We confirm whether your profile meets Polish consulate standards before you pay any visa fees.

2

Work Permit / Declaration

Your employer registers the work permit or Declaration with the Polish Voivodeship office. This takes 7–30 days depending on the region. GVJ guides your employer through this process.

3

Document Pack Built

We compile your complete application — financial evidence, cover letter, insurance, accommodation proof — aligned to the specific consulate requirements in your country.

4

Consulate Submission

You attend the Polish consulate appointment. Processing time is typically 15–45 days. We prepare you for the interview and answer any queries the consulate raises.

5

Visa Issued — Depart

Visa collected, pre-departure briefing completed. You travel to Poland and begin work. Your Type D visa also allows 90 days of travel within the wider Schengen area.

Route Two

UK Seasonal Worker Visa

The UK Seasonal Worker visa allows nationals of eligible countries — including Nigeria, Ghana, and other West African nations — to work in UK horticulture and food processing for up to 6 months. Placement is arranged through a licensed UK scheme operator who issues your Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS). This is currently one of the most accessible and fastest UK work visa routes available.

Key Facts

Visa Type

Seasonal Worker

Max Duration

6 months

Visa Fee

£298

Processing Time

3–4 weeks

Sectors

Horticulture / Food

Sponsor Needed

Yes — licensed CoS

Documents Required

Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond your planned departure date)
Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) reference number from your licensed scheme operator
Evidence of sufficient personal funds (at least £1,270 in your bank account)
Tuberculosis (TB) test certificate (for Nigerian nationals)
Bank statements — 28 consecutive days showing the required balance
Completed online visa application and biometric enrolment at a visa centre

Application Timeline

1

Profile & Eligibility Check

We review your passport, bank statements, and travel history. We confirm you meet the funds requirement and flag any issues — including the TB test if required — before any fees are paid.

2

Scheme Placement & CoS

A licensed scheme operator assigns you a placement and issues your Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS). GVJ works with operators to ensure your CoS is correctly structured for your application.

3

Application & Document Pack

We complete your online application, organise your 28-day bank statements in the correct format, prepare your funds evidence, and book your biometric appointment at a UK Visas centre.

4

Biometrics & Submission

You attend your biometrics appointment and your application is submitted. UK Visas & Immigration typically processes within 3 weeks. We track progress and respond to any UKVI queries.

5

Visa Issued — Depart to UK

Your vignette visa is issued. We provide a pre-departure briefing — what to expect at UK border control, your rights as a seasonal worker, and how to access your BRP on arrival.

Ready to Start?

The key hurdle on the Seasonal Worker route is the funds evidence. Many applicants are rejected because their bank statements don't meet UKVI's 28-day rule. We check this before you apply — not after.

Common Questions

Work Visa FAQ

The Type D is Poland's national long-stay visa, valid for up to 12 months. It is issued to non-EU nationals who have a confirmed job offer or work permit from a Polish employer. It covers office roles, skilled trades, logistics, and other sectors — not just agriculture. The visa also gives you 90 days of travel within the wider Schengen zone, making Poland one of the most attractive mid-term work destinations for West African professionals.
The UK Seasonal Worker visa is open to nationals of most countries, including Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, and other West African nations. You must have a Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) from a Home Office-licensed scheme operator, hold at least £1,270 in your bank account for 28 consecutive days, and have a valid passport. Nigerian nationals must also complete a TB test at an approved clinic before applying.
Yes — both routes require employer documentation before you can apply for the visa. For Poland, you need a work permit or Declaration of Entrusting Work issued by your employer. For the UK Seasonal Worker route, you need a CoS from a licensed scheme operator. If you do not yet have an employer, contact us via WhatsApp. We can advise on reputable operators and schemes, though we do not guarantee job placement.
For the UK Seasonal Worker route, the online application is typically decided within 3–4 weeks once all documents are submitted. For the Poland Type D, the consulate process takes 15–45 days after submission, but you should allow an additional 4–8 weeks for the work permit to be issued by the Polish labour authority first. We recommend starting at least 3 months before your intended start date.
A previous Schengen or UK refusal does not automatically disqualify you, but it must be disclosed and addressed directly in your application. The reason for refusal matters significantly — financial evidence failures, ties to home country concerns, and travel history issues can all be mitigated with the right preparation. Contact us for a personal assessment before applying. Do not apply without proper preparation after a refusal — a second refusal is harder to recover from.
The UK Seasonal Worker visa does not allow dependants. For the Poland Type D, dependants can apply for a separate family reunification visa once you are legally resident in Poland, but this is a separate process and not guaranteed. If family reunification is a priority, discuss this with us at the assessment stage so we can factor it into the route planning.
